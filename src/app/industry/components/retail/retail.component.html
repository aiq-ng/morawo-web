Application of data science in retail

• Dynamic Pricing: Adjust prices to the characteristics and demand behavior of individual customers or groups.

• Sales Forecasting: We develop models to predict the amount sold for your products and product groups.

• Disposition optimization: Modeling of order behavior at store level to avoid out-of-stock situations. 

• Customized prices: Determination of customer-specific price elasticities and demand functions using choice sets.

• Up- und Cross-Selling: Analysis of historical shopping carts to generate product recommendations and purchase advice.

• Price elasticities: Statistical modeling of price-sales functions on store for optimal pricing.

Data analysis in Retail

Many retailers already recognize the impact that big data has on customer service, supply chain optimization, and business strategies. Big data in retail offers stores the opportunity to learn and capitalize on market trends to increase profitability.

Retail data analytics involves collecting big data to improve pricing strategies, supply chain control, and the customer experience. Big data refers to market collections and internal company information that reveal global industry and customer trends. Scientists traditionally define big data by evaluating its volume, speed, and variety. In other words, the data collection must be large, fast to process, and diverse. These characteristics make it more valuable to the company.

Big data in the retail industry helps companies improve their customer service. Retailers can use valuable customer data to improve product development, the shopping journey, and the overall buyer experience.
